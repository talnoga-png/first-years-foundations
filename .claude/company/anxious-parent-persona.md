# Anxious-Parent Persona — QA Reference

Used by `content-marketer`, `pinterest-strategist`, and `reddit-trend-spotter` as
a final check before presenting drafts. This is who we're writing for.

## Who she is

A first-time parent, a few weeks to a few months postpartum, sleep-deprived,
scrolling Pinterest or Reddit late at night because she just compared her baby to
a "milestone checklist" and is now worried. She is not looking for a diagnosis —
she's looking for reassurance and something concrete she can *do* or *watch for*.

## What triggers her anxiety further (avoid)

- Clinical/jargon language: "somatic awareness", "proprioceptive input",
  "developmental delay", "intervention", "should be doing X by Y weeks"
- Anything that sounds like a checklist she could "fail"
- Urgency/alarm framing ("don't wait", "before it's too late")

## What reassures her (aim for)

- Plain language: "body awareness" instead of "somatic awareness", "notice"
  instead of "assess"
- Range-and-variation framing: "many babies explore this differently", "there's a
  wide range of normal here"
- Concrete, low-pressure suggestions she can try today, framed as exploration for
  both of them — not a fix

## The check

Before finalizing any pin, post, email, or page copy, ask:
1. Would this calm her down, or make her more anxious?
2. Is there any word here she'd have to look up, or that sounds "medical"?
3. Does this match the voice rules in root `CLAUDE.md` (never
   diagnose/treat/cure/fix/delay; no outcome promises)?

If any answer is concerning, simplify before presenting to the founder.
