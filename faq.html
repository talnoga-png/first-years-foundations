Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our guides and methods

+ What is the Feldenkrais method?

The Feldenkrais method is an educational approach to human movement and learning developed by physicist and martial artist Moshֳ© Feldenkrais. Rather than teaching the "right" way to move, the Feldenkrais method invites exploration of gentle movements and self-awareness. This approach helps your nervous system learn more efficient, comfortable patterns of movement and coordination. For babies and young children, Feldenkrais principles emphasize patient observation and low-pressure environments where your baby's natural development can unfold.

+ Are your guides based on medical research?

Our guides are informed by the Feldenkrais method, developmental observation, and years of hands-on experience with babies and families. They are educational resources, not medical guidelines. We always encourage you to consult with your pediatrician if you have concerns about your baby's health or development. Our approach complements, rather than replaces, professional medical care.

+ Do I need any special training to use these guides?

No. The guides are written for parents without any special training or background knowledge. Each idea is explained clearly and includes simple ways to observe your baby during everyday moments. The goal is to deepen your natural connection with your baby while exploring movement together.

+ Can I use multiple guides at once?

Yes, absolutely. Many families find it helpful to explore ideas from their baby's current stage while also looking ahead to what's coming next. You could also revisit earlier guides if your baby seems interested in those movements. There's no prescribed orderג€”use them in the way that works best for your family.

+ What if my baby doesn't seem interested?

That's completely normal. Every baby is unique, and readiness for movement exploration varies. The guides are designed to invite exploration, not demand it. If an idea doesn't resonate with your baby today, you can revisit it laterג€”sometimes a small change in timing, position, or approach makes all the difference.

+ Are the guides suitable for babies with developmental delays?

Our guides are educational resources for typical development. If your baby has been identified with developmental delays or concerns, we recommend working with a physical therapist, occupational therapist, or early intervention specialist who can tailor strategies to your baby's specific needs. These professionals can help you understand when and how to use exploratory movement practices safely and effectively.
