# Lawyer Review Checklist

Before launch, have an attorney review the legal/compliance pages. Use this checklist to guide their review.

**Jurisdiction Note:** US-based (English-speaking), targeting parents. Business model: digital PDF sales via Whop, email marketing via MailerLite.

## Pages Requiring Legal Review

- [ ] `disclaimer.html` — Medical/liability disclaimers
- [ ] `terms.html` — Terms of use, licensing, purchase terms, refund policy
- [ ] `privacy.html` — Privacy policy, data collection, GDPR/CCPA compliance

## Key Legal Considerations

### Disclaimer Page

**To Verify:**
- [ ] Liability waiver adequately protects against injury claims from suggested activities?
- [ ] "Assumption of Risk" section is enforceable in your state/jurisdiction?
- [ ] Medical disclaimer language is sufficient to distinguish educational vs. medical advice?
- [ ] "Not a Substitute for Professional Care" list covers all relevant professional types?
- [ ] Any missing liability scenarios (e.g., choking hazards, fall risk from rolling)?

**Standard Review:**
- Language appropriately strong without being threatening/off-putting?
- All key disclaimers appear on product pages (currently in footer only)?

---

### Terms of Use Page

**Purchase & Licensing:**
- [ ] "Non-exclusive, personal use only" license is appropriate for $14.99–$39.99 digital products?
- [ ] Prohibited uses (resale, distribution, commercial use) are clearly defined?
- [ ] Refund policy ("all sales final, no refunds") complies with payment processor (Whop) requirements?

**Intellectual Property:**
- [ ] Copyright notice and protection language is adequate?
- [ ] Any concerns with how PDFs are delivered/protected (DRM, watermarking)?

**Limitation of Liability:**
- [ ] "To the fullest extent permitted by law" language is enforceable in your jurisdiction?
- [ ] Exclusion of consequential/indirect damages is appropriate?
- [ ] Any gaps in covered scenarios (medical injury, emotional distress, etc.)?

**Third-Party Links:**
- [ ] Disclaimer for Whop and MailerLite links is sufficient?
- [ ] Any additional terms needed for affiliate/revenue sharing?

---

### Privacy Policy Page

**Data Collection:**
- [ ] All data collection methods disclosed (name, email, payment info, IP address, browsing behavior)?
- [ ] GA4 tracking disclosed and user consent compliant (GDPR if applicable)?
- [ ] MailerLite data handling and retention practices accurately described?

**GDPR/CCPA Compliance** (if applicable to your audience):
- [ ] Right to access, delete, update personal data clearly stated?
- [ ] Opt-out from marketing emails available?
- [ ] Data processing agreements in place with Whop, MailerLite, GA4?

**Children's Privacy (COPPA):**
- [ ] Clarification that site is for adults only (parents/caregivers)?
- [ ] Acknowledgment that minors shouldn't be providing data?

**Third-Party Services:**
- [ ] Whop, MailerLite, GA4, GitHub Pages privacy policies linked or referenced?
- [ ] Accuracy of how third parties use user data?

**Data Security:**
- [ ] Representations about encryption/security are accurate and not over-promised?
- [ ] Any liability limitations for data breaches?

---

## Standard Business Questions

1. **Business Structure** — Are you operating as sole proprietor, LLC, S-corp? Terms should reflect liability protection if incorporated.

2. **Sales Tax** — Any sales tax collection needed for US states? (Note: PDF sales may be exempt in some states, but varies.)

3. **Payment Processor Terms** — Does Whop's ToS conflict with any of your terms (especially refunds, chargeback policies)?

4. **Email Marketing Compliance** — MailerLite GDPR-compliant? Opt-in/unsubscribe mechanisms in place?

5. **Seller Information** — Should legal pages list business name, address, owner name? (Currently only email: hello@first-year-foundations.com)
