The Feldenkrais method is an educational approach to human movement and learning developed by physicist and martial artist Mosh Feldenkrais. What makes it distinctive is its holistic view of movement: it doesn't treat the body as a collection of muscles and joints to train in isolation. Instead, it sees movement as a whole-system process — one that involves the nervous system, the skeleton, the senses, and the environment working together. Rather than teaching the "right" way to move, the Feldenkrais method invites gentle exploration and self-awareness, letting the nervous system discover more efficient and comfortable patterns on its own terms. For babies, this means observing what they're already doing, creating conditions that invite more, and trusting that development unfolds when a baby feels safe, supported, and free to explore.

Most baby development resources are built around milestone checklists — a list of things your baby "should" be doing by a certain age. We think that misses the point. Milestones are outcomes. What actually shapes development is the quality of movement that comes before them: how freely your baby can explore, how comfortable they feel in their body, how much varied floor experience they get. Our guides focus on that — the underlying movement quality — because that's where the Feldenkrais method operates. We're a group of Feldenkrais-certified movement practitioners. Movement isn't a topic we write about; it's the expertise we've built through years of direct work with babies and families. The result is guidance that's grounded in a deep, holistic understanding of how human movement actually develops — not just a checklist of what to do by when.
