Why First Year Foundations?

Most baby development advice is asking the wrong question

Milestone checklists tell you whether your baby is rolling, sitting, or crawling by a certain age. They don't tell you what actually makes those things possible.

The Feldenkrais Method starts from a different premise: movement isn't a set of skills to drill into a baby. It's a whole-system process — one that involves the nervous system, the skeleton, the senses, and the environment working together. What shapes development isn't hitting the "right" movements on schedule. It's the quality of the movement environment your baby lives in day to day.

That's what our guides are built around. Not "is your baby on track?" but "what conditions let natural development unfold?" This holistic view — movement as learning, not performance — changes what you pay attention to, how you set up your baby's environment, and how you think about the time you spend with them on the floor.

And it comes from people for whom movement is their life's work: a group of Feldenkrais-certified practitioners with 15+ years of hands-on experience with babies and families.
